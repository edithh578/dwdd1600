Floral Care

Floral care is essential to ensure that your flowers remain vibrant and last as long as possible. Proper care begins with choosing fresh, healthy blooms.

Category Step One Step Two Step Three
Watering Use clean, room-temperature water. Keep the water level about 2/3 of the stem length. Change the water every 2-3 days to keep it fresh.
Vase Preparation Clean the vase thoroughly before use. Ensure there is no bacteria or mold in the vase. Fill the vase with enough water to support the flowers.
Stem Care Trim stems at a 45-degree angle to improve absorption. Cut under water to prevent air bubbles. Re-trim stems every 2-3 days to extend flower life.
Light & Temperature Keep flowers in indirect light. Avoid heat sources like direct sunlight. Place flowers in a cool area, avoiding drafts.
Flower Types Some flowers, like roses, need more water. Certain flowers, like tulips, prefer cooler temps. Research specific needs for each flower variety.
